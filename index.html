Project Plan — TerraIntel v3 Dark Edition

Challenge response

TerraIntel uses NASA Earth observation data to guide smart urban growth that supports both people and the planet. Integrating datasets such as Landsat (land cover & change), VIIRS Night Lights (urban activity), GRACE (groundwater), GPM (precipitation), and NASA POWER (surface climate) provides city planners with actionable insights on how rapid urbanization affects ecosystems, natural resources, and infrastructure.

By mapping urban expansion, resource stress, and ecosystem health, we can identify sustainable development zones, prioritize green infrastructure, and prevent environmental degradation while meeting human needs. City leaders — including planning departments, parks, water utilities, and transportation agencies — will use these insights to coordinate investments, set zoning priorities, and design equitable service delivery. Residents contribute local knowledge through participatory reporting (e.g., mobile apps, community surveys) to complement satellite observations and validate priorities identified by the system.

How datasets support plan topics

  • Access & Housing: Landsat + VIIRS help detect built-area expansion and nighttime economic activity, guiding where new housing and services should be prioritized. City housing offices and community groups collaborate to site affordable housing near transit and services.
  • Food & Local Agriculture: NDVI (MODIS/Landsat), GPM rainfall, and GRACE water storage identify productive parcels suitable for urban agriculture, supported by the parks and urban agriculture offices.
  • Mobility & Services: VIIRS + population grids (SEDAC) show activity centers and under-served corridors; transportation agencies use this to prioritize routes and last-mile solutions.

City leadership & community role

City leaders operationalize TerraIntel by embedding data dashboards into planning workflows, convening cross-departmental working groups, and allocating budget to priority projects identified by the platform. Residents validate and refine priorities through community reporting tools and participatory workshops, ensuring the plan reflects lived experiences and local constraints.

TerraIntel therefore frames a pragmatic, data-informed approach to grow cities sustainably while protecting natural capital and improving quality of life.
